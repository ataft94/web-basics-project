Dinner Party w/ Alex


Background

Several years ago, I offered to cook dinner for my parents on my dad's birthday - which just so happens to be Christmas eve. He loves food, but because of my mom's dietary restrictions, he mostly eats makes meals that tend towards beige and starch. I decided to develop a full menu and invite some friends of his. Ever since, my dad has requested a new menu every year and we've made it a tradition to cook together and share techniques and culinary knowledge. It felt like a gift we could both enjoy.

Last year, we discovered my grandmother Carolyn's infamous index card boxes where she kept her recipes from when she married my grandfather in the Italian Alps just days after the end of WWII to when she died at the age of 94 in 2012. These green tin boxes were stuffed to the gills with carefully handwritten notes, transcribed recipes, and magazine cutouts carefully pasted to the cardstock. Some of them don't necessarily stand the test of time (what is "oriental chicken?") but they reflect a life of thinking out how to prepare and serve food to people you love.
